# Product

## Register

product

## Users

One user: a high-school founder/student who runs PiOS 24/7 on a Raspberry Pi beside their main monitor. Uses it at-a-glance dozens of times per day — to check what needs attention, capture tasks, and track projects and school deadlines without opening another tab.

## Product Purpose

PiOS is a personal command center, not a dashboard. Its job is to eliminate the context switches caused by constantly checking GitHub, Gmail, task lists, assignment trackers, and college apps separately. Every element must answer: what needs my attention right now?

## Brand Personality

Sharp · Calm · Precise. A mission-control room designed by Apple. Quiet authority — never shouts, never decorates. Information at a glance, zero friction.

## Anti-references

- **Notion**: soft, document-like, excessive whitespace, airy — the opposite of dense and precise
- **Generic SaaS dark**: indigo/purple gradients, glassmorphism cards, hero metrics, decorative glow
- **Grafana**: chart overload, data for data's sake
- **Home Assistant**: consumer tile grids, colorful icons

## Design Principles

1. **Attention over aesthetics** — every visual choice serves the question "what needs my attention?" If it doesn't answer that, remove it.
2. **Density is respect** — the user is capable; don't waste space on padding and decoration they didn't ask for.
3. **Calm authority** — the interface should feel like a cockpit: precise, purposeful, never decorative.
4. **One voice, two registers** — the same design system speaks for both Projects (startup energy) and School (deadline discipline).
5. **OS, not website** — no scroll, no hero sections, no marketing conventions. Think menubar, panels, and status indicators.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Body text ≥4.5:1 contrast. Reduced motion honored. Single user, so no i18n needed.
